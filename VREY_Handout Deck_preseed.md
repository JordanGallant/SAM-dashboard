## 4. Competition

Existing solutions are **outdated or misaligned** with landlord needs. Market fragmentation compounds the problem -- **No integrated solution** available.

### Smart Metering
- **Significantly outdated:** Default metering point operators (MPO) are unable to provide smart metering infrastructure (40% of all MPOs haven't installed one). Those who have, lack the system and skill for data processing.
- **Do not fit landlord needs:** Independent MPOs are expensive given their blue-collar exposure. They do not support the new regulatory framework (S42b EnWG) due to system requirements and process capabilities.
- Competitors: e-netz, stadtwerke, inexogy, metiundo...

### Renewable Production
- **Significantly outdated:** Hardware-first solutions were created before a regulatory framework to sell solar locally existed. With the new regulation, they are now obsolete and come with unjustified costs and hardware.
- **Do not fit landlord needs:** Software-first solutions have invested heavily in a different regulatory framework that fits different needs. Roof-leasing players offer significantly worse project profitability to landlords.
- Competitors: Pionierkraft, WEISS, solarize, Marcley, ALVA, Enpal, metergrid...

### Flexible Consumption
- **Significantly outdated:** Default MPOs are unable to provide the required control box and respective services for landlords to receive reduced grid fees.
- **Do not fit landlord needs:** Single-family energy management systems do not support apartment building applications that require intelligence on the behavior of multiple tenants and the allocation of renewable production.
- Competitors: e-netz, stadtwerke, gridX...

**No integration** between these three areas -- VREY is the only integrated solution.
